Fernando Amorsolo y Cueto

One of the Most Important Artist

Portraitist and Painter of rural Philippine Landscape

The Life and Paintings

  • Life and Education - Born in May 30, 1892, in Paco Manila. Don Fabian de la Rosa, his mother's cousin also a filipino Painter who guided him to his painting career. At the age of 13, he became an apprentice to De la Rosa. He has a brother named Pablo Amorsolo, also a painter. He entered in University of the Philippines' School of Fine Arts, where his mentor De la Rosa worked at that time. After graduting from University of the Philippines, he works as a chief artist of the Pacific Commercial Company, and a part-time instructor at University.After 3 years of as an intructor he got a privilege to study at Acadamia de San Fernando in Madrid. Later he travel to New York City and encounted impressionism and cubism.

  • Style and Landscapes - Amorsolo is the best known for his illuminated landscapes, he often potrayed traditional Filipino customs, culture and fiestas. He was learned in classical tradition and achieve his own version of Greek ideal for his human form. Amorsolo used a natural light in his paintings and develop the blacklighting techniques Chiaroscuro, become his artistic trademark and his greatest contibution to Philippine painting. His typical painting outlined against a characteristic glow, and intense light on one part of the canvas highlights. Philippine sunlight was a constant feature of Amorsolo; he believed to have painted only one rainy-day scene.

  • Contemporary Filipino Arts - Amorsolo was an important influence on contemporary Filipino art and artist, even beyond the so-called "Amorsolo School" Amorsolo honored as the First National Artist in Painting at the Cultural Center of the Philippines by President Ferdinand Marcos. The volume of paintings have 10,000 pieces. In 2003, Amorsolo's children founded the Fernando C. Amorsolo Art Foundation, which is dedicated to preserving Fernando Amorsolo Legacy.
